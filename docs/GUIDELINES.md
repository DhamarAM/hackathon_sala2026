# SALA 2026 Hackathon — Guidelines

## 1. Team Formation

- **Team size:** minimum 4, maximum 8 members. No solo participation.
- **Interdisciplinary teams** strongly encouraged (data science, domain expertise, design, coding).
- **Find teammates:** SALA 2026 WhatsApp community team-formation chat.
- **Team Lead:** each team must designate one lead responsible for all official communication and final submission.
- **Register:** use the official form — same form for slides submission.

---

## 2. Formulating Your Research Question

Two paths:

**Path A — Pre-determined Problems**
Choose from SALA's curated list. Comes with pre-selected data subsets focused on marine biology and climate monitoring in the Galápagos.

**Path B — Define Your Own**
Must be clear, measurable, and address a specific ML/AI problem.

### Criteria for a Strong Research Question
- **Clarity and Focus:** easily understood, one central idea
- **Challenge:** appropriate for ML techniques
- **Relevance:** aligned with the hackathon spirit, uses provided or public data
- **Feasibility:** prototype-able within the hackathon period

---

## 3. Data & Tools

- **Data access:** all official datasets hosted on Cloudflare — credentials shared with registered teams
- **Permitted:** SALA datasets + publicly available external sources (legally obtained, properly cited)
- **Forbidden:** proprietary or non-public external data
- **Tools:** any language or library (Python, R, SQL, etc.)

---

## 4. Pitch Slide Deck

- **Format:** 3-minute live presentation to the judging panel
- **No template** — use any software (Google Slides, PowerPoint, Canva)
- **Maximum 5 slides:**

| Slide | Content |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Project Title, Team Name, Team Members |
| 2 | Problem Statement & Research Question |
| 3 | Data & Methodology |
| 4 | Results & Prototype / MVP |
| 5 | Conclusion & Future Vision |
| (optional) | Citations |
